# GP Scoring Rubric — 12 dimensions

Use this rubric when producing the five-lens scorecard. Each dimension gets a 1-5 score with weighted aggregate.

## Scoring scale (apply to every dimension)
- **5** — Best-in-class. Clear evidence, third-party validation, sustained over time.
- **4** — Strong. Above-median performance with credible evidence.
- **3** — Acceptable. Meets institutional standards with no major concerns.
- **2** — Below the bar. Material concerns; commit only with mitigations.
- **1** — Disqualifying. Do not commit.

A score of 1 on any single dimension is a veto, regardless of aggregate.

## The 12 dimensions

### 1. Team continuity (weight: 12%)
- Years of GPs working together
- Partner turnover rate in last 5 years
- Key-person coverage breadth (how many partners must depart to trigger key-person)
- Evidence of succession planning

### 2. Attribution clarity (weight: 10%)
- Deal-by-deal attribution available
- Partner-level returns visible
- Sourcing vs underwriting vs portfolio support attribution
- Honest accounting of "the founder did the magic" risk

### 3. Track record quality (weight: 15%)
- DPI relative to vintage benchmark
- Net IRR vintage quartile (top, second, third, fourth)
- PME above public market alternative (positive alpha)
- Loss ratio vs strategy norm
- Concentration of returns (top-3-deal contribution to TVPI)

### 4. Strategy distinctiveness (weight: 10%)
- One-sentence thesis articulation passes the sniff test
- Named competitors with clear differentiation
- Pipeline / sourcing edge demonstrated, not asserted
- Strategy consistency across funds OR coherent evolution

### 5. Capital absorption (weight: 8%)
- Fund size appropriate for strategy
- Check size discipline ($X to $Y range, no drift)
- Reserves allocation consistent with strategy
- AUM not exceeding cognitive / operational capacity

### 6. Value-add evidence (weight: 8%)
- Portfolio company CEO references positive
- Specific examples of value created (not just "we helped")
- Operating partner / advisor depth real, not theater
- For impact-aligned: value-add includes impact-as-a-service, not just selection

### 7. Economic terms (weight: 8%)
- Management fee competitive for fund size & strategy
- Carry structure (European waterfall preferred)
- Hurdle rate appropriate
- GP commitment % aligned

### 8. Governance terms (weight: 7%)
- Key-person clause adequate
- No-fault divorce / removal terms
- LP Advisory Committee structure
- Reporting transparency (ILPA-compliant)

### 9. GP economics & succession (weight: 5%)
- GP equity distribution among partners
- Management company economics not extractive
- Carry split aligns with team contribution
- Generational handoff plan if relevant

### 10. Operational sophistication (weight: 6%)
- Back-office quality (audit, tax, compliance)
- Reporting timeliness and detail
- IR responsiveness
- Cyber / data security posture

### 11. Portfolio construction fit (weight: 7%)
- Vintage diversification benefit
- Sector / geographic concentration impact
- Single-manager exposure within limits
- Co-invest pipeline if relevant

### 12. Impact / mission alignment (weight: 4% baseline, 15%+ for impact mandates)
- Impact thesis credibility (route to `impact-diligence` skill)
- Mission lock / governance protections
- Negative externality awareness
- Reporting compatibility with FoF impact reporting

## Aggregation guidance

- Apply weights, compute weighted average
- **Weighted score ≥ 4.0** → strong commit candidate
- **3.5-4.0** → commit with named conditions
- **3.0-3.5** → more work needed, do not commit yet
- **< 3.0** → pass
- **Any dimension at 1** → veto regardless of aggregate

## Weighting adjustments by mandate

| Mandate type | Adjust upward | Adjust downward |
|---|---|---|
| Emerging manager program | Team continuity, attribution clarity | Track record |
| Re-up decision | Strategy distinctiveness, value-add | Team continuity (already known) |
| Impact / regen / natural capital | Impact alignment (to 15-20%), governance | Capital absorption |
| Secondaries | Track record, terms | Strategy distinctiveness |
| Co-invest | Value-add, attribution | Economic / governance terms |
