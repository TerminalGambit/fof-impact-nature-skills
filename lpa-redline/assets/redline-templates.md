# Redline Templates — LP-favorable drafting notes

Drafting notes for the ten most-negotiated LPA clauses. These are **drafting notes for legal counsel**, not final legal text. Adapt the substance to the specific draft. Always route the final LPA through fund-formation counsel before signature.

Each template follows the same shape:
- **Issue** — what's being negotiated
- **GP-friendly starting point** — typical draft language
- **LP-favorable position** — what to ask for
- **Market-standard fallback** — where most LPs settle
- **Rationale** — the ILPA / market argument

---

## 1. Management fee step-down

**Issue:** When does the basis shift from committed capital to invested capital (or NAV)?

**GP-friendly starting point:** Fee continues on committed capital throughout the fund life, or step-down delayed until a successor fund's final close.

**LP-favorable position:** Step-down to invested capital (net of write-offs and write-downs, and net of capital returned) effective on the earlier of (a) the end of the investment period or (b) the first close of any successor fund pursuing a substantially similar strategy.

**Market-standard fallback:** Step-down to invested capital effective at end of investment period, with successor-fund close trigger negotiable.

**Rationale:** ILPA Principles 3.0 treat the post-investment-period fee on committed capital as economically unjustified — the GP is no longer sourcing new investments, so the fee base should track deployed capital actually being managed.

---

## 2. Carry waterfall (European vs American)

**Issue:** Is carry paid only after all LP capital plus preferred return is returned (European / whole-fund), or deal-by-deal subject to clawback (American)?

**GP-friendly starting point:** American waterfall, deal-by-deal, with limited clawback.

**LP-favorable position:** European whole-fund waterfall. All contributed capital plus preferred return returned before any carry is distributed to GP.

**Market-standard fallback:** If American is non-negotiable (common in US venture), insist on (a) full clawback gross of taxes with interest, (b) escrow of 25-30% of GP distributions until final liquidation, (c) joint-and-several GP backstop.

**Rationale:** Whole-fund waterfall aligns GP economics to total-fund performance, not selection of early winners. American waterfalls create incentive to harvest winners early and ride losses, which is misaligned with LP outcomes.

---

## 3. Clawback

**Issue:** If GP receives carry on early winners and later losers wipe out fund-level returns, how is excess carry recovered?

**GP-friendly starting point:** Clawback at fund termination, net of taxes paid by GP partners, no interest, no security.

**LP-favorable position:** Full clawback gross of taxes (i.e., GP returns full dollar amount, not net of personal taxes), with interest at fund's preferred return rate, secured by escrow of 25-30% of distributions and a joint-and-several guarantee from carry recipients.

**Market-standard fallback:** Gross-of-taxes clawback with interest at risk-free rate, escrow of 20-25%, several-only guarantee.

**Rationale:** Net-of-tax clawback transfers tax timing and rate risk to the LP — the LP funded the GP's full pre-tax carry but receives back only the post-tax amount. ILPA Principles 3.0 endorse gross clawback as the LP norm.

---

## 4. Key-person clause

**Issue:** Which partners trigger investment-period suspension if they leave or reduce time commitment, and how is the suspension lifted?

**GP-friendly starting point:** Single named partner, suspension auto-lifts after 6 months or on GP-designated replacement.

**LP-favorable position:** At least 2-3 named "Key Persons" with a substantially-all-business-time obligation. If any one departs, dies, or reduces time below the standard, investment period automatically suspends. Suspension lifted only by LP-majority vote (or LPAC supermajority) approving the replacement. Suspension remains in place during the search.

**Market-standard fallback:** 2 named keys, "majority leave" trigger, suspension lifted by LPAC supermajority within a defined window (e.g., 180 days), otherwise terminates investment period.

**Rationale:** LPs are underwriting specific individuals, not the firm brand. Single-name keys create single-point-of-failure risk. Auto-lifting suspension neutralizes the protection.

---

## 5. No-fault removal

**Issue:** What threshold removes the GP without cause?

**GP-friendly starting point:** 80%+ of LP interests required, by headcount or by combined capital and headcount; removal triggers termination payment to GP.

**LP-favorable position:** 75% in interest of LPs (by committed capital, excluding GP and affiliates from the denominator); on removal, GP forfeits all unvested carry; no termination payment.

**Market-standard fallback:** 75-80% in interest, with limited GP transition payment capped at unreimbursed expenses.

**Rationale:** No-fault removal is the LPs' ultimate governance backstop. Setting threshold too high or by headcount makes it unexercisable. ILPA Principles 3.0 standard is 75% in interest.

---

## 6. Recycling cap

**Issue:** Can the GP redeploy distributions back into new investments, and at what cap?

**GP-friendly starting point:** Uncapped recycling during the fund life, including return-of-capital distributions and follow-ons.

**LP-favorable position:** Recycling capped at 110% of commitments. Only short-term realizations (within 18 months of investment) eligible for recycling. Recycling only permitted during the investment period. No recycling of return-of-capital distributions or bridge-loan repayments. LPs receive notice of recycling decisions.

**Market-standard fallback:** 110-125% cap on recycling, 24-month short-realization window, recycling within investment period only.

**Rationale:** Uncapped recycling extends LP exposure beyond commitment without giving LPs the chance to evaluate vintage and pacing. Recycling caps preserve commitment discipline.

---

## 7. MFN (Most Favored Nation)

**Issue:** Which LPs see and can elect into other LPs' side-letter terms?

**GP-friendly starting point:** Tiered MFN with broad carve-outs that exclude the LP from meaningful terms; short election period; redacted disclosure.

**LP-favorable position:** All side letters fully disclosed (subject only to identity redaction where required by regulation). MFN tiers, if any, set by genuine commitment-size brackets — not designed to exclude. Election period 30 calendar days from receipt of complete package. Carve-outs limited to bona fide regulatory (ERISA, public-pension public-records, sovereign), and these must be specifically identified, not blanket.

**Market-standard fallback:** Tiered MFN with the LP in a tier where the meaningful economic and governance terms are accessible; 30-day election; regulatory-only carve-outs.

**Rationale:** MFN is the LP's protection against bilateral GP discrimination. Broad carve-outs hollow it out. ILPA Principles 3.0 emphasize transparency and meaningful election.

---

## 8. LPAC powers

**Issue:** What does the LP Advisory Committee actually decide, versus advise on?

**GP-friendly starting point:** LPAC is purely advisory; GP retains all decision authority subject to disclosure.

**LP-favorable position:** LPAC has binding approval rights over: (a) all affiliate and cross-fund transactions, (b) any change in valuation policy or valuation of unrealized assets above a materiality threshold, (c) any change in key persons or investment-period suspension lifting, (d) all extensions of the fund or investment period, (e) any GP-led secondary or continuation-vehicle transaction (with independent fairness opinion), (f) any amendment to the conflicts policy or investment guidelines, (g) any side-letter term that would otherwise breach pari-passu treatment.

**Market-standard fallback:** Binding approval on (a), (c), (d), (e); advisory with mandatory consultation on (b), (f), (g).

**Rationale:** LPAC is the LPs' only continuous governance forum during the fund's life. Without binding rights over conflict and value-impacting decisions, it's window dressing.

---

## 9. Successor-fund restriction

**Issue:** When can the GP begin marketing the next fund in the same strategy?

**GP-friendly starting point:** GP may market a successor fund at any time, subject only to disclosure.

**LP-favorable position:** No marketing of a successor fund pursuing a substantially similar strategy until the earlier of (a) 75% of commitments are invested, committed, or reserved for follow-ons and expenses, or (b) end of the investment period. Key persons subject to substantially-all-business-time on this fund through the investment period or until the 75% threshold is reached.

**Market-standard fallback:** 70% threshold; key persons exclusive through investment period.

**Rationale:** Premature successor-fund fundraising creates competition for the GP's attention and may degrade execution on the current fund. ILPA Principles 3.0 endorse a deployment-threshold gate on successor marketing.

---

## 10. Impact mission lock

**Issue:** What prevents drift away from the stated impact thesis over the fund's life?

**GP-friendly starting point:** Impact thesis described in the PPM but not embedded in the LPA; GP retains full discretion to interpret and adjust.

**LP-favorable position:** Impact thesis (theory of change, target outcomes, target beneficiaries, geography) stated in LPA recitals and incorporated by reference into the investment guidelines. Negative screens (exclusion list) set out in a schedule to the LPA. Impact reporting standard specified (IRIS+, GIIN Operating Principles, or named framework) with cadence and verification requirements. Amendment to recitals, exclusion list, or reporting standard requires supermajority LP consent (e.g., 67% in interest). Persistent failure to meet stated impact KPIs (e.g., two consecutive annual reports below threshold) triggers mandatory LPAC review and may, on LP-majority vote, constitute cause for investment-period suspension.

**Market-standard fallback:** Recitals reference, schedule for negative screens, named impact framework, LPAC review on persistent KPI miss (without removal trigger).

**Rationale:** Impact mandates require structural protection against drift, particularly as fund size grows or market conditions tighten. Without LPA-level mission lock, the impact thesis is unenforceable by LPs and unprotected against staffing or strategy changes.

---

## How to use these templates

1. Read the GP's draft for the clause in question.
2. Identify which template applies (or several, since clauses interact — e.g., carry + clawback).
3. Use the **LP-favorable position** as your opening ask in the redline.
4. Use the **market-standard fallback** as your second-position settlement.
5. Use the **rationale** as the substance of the negotiation email or call.
6. Adapt the substance to the specific draft — do not paste any of this into the LPA as final legal text.

**Always route the final LPA through legal counsel. This skill identifies negotiation positions; it does not replace fund-formation legal review.**
