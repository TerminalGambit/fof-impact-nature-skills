# TNFD priority sectors — quick reference

TNFD's sector guidance (built on SASB sub-industries and ENCORE materiality ratings) identifies sectors where nature-related dependencies and impacts are systematically material. When a deal sits in one of these, deeper LEAP application is warranted by default — and disclosure preparers should expect higher scrutiny from regulators, LPs, and civil society.

This list aggregates TNFD's priority sectors into 14 working categories. Use it as a screening filter at the start of LEAP-Locate.

## The 14 priority sectors

### 1. Food, agriculture, beverages
- Crop production, livestock, aquaculture, dairy, prepared foods, alcoholic beverages, non-alcoholic beverages
- **Typical dependencies:** soil quality regulation, water supply, pollination, climate regulation, pest & disease control, genetic resources
- **Typical impacts:** land-use change (conversion), water abstraction, nutrient and pesticide pollution, GHG emissions, soil degradation
- **LEAP focus:** site- and supply-shed-level deforestation risk, water stress, pollination dependence, regenerative transition evidence

### 2. Forestry, pulp & paper
- Forest management, timber, pulp, paper, packaging
- **Typical dependencies:** soil quality, water supply, climate regulation, pest & disease control, genetic resources
- **Typical impacts:** land-use change, primary forest loss, biodiversity loss, soil disturbance
- **LEAP focus:** FSC/PEFC coverage, primary vs plantation footprint, EUDR exposure

### 3. Mining & metals
- Coal, metal ores, precious metals, construction materials extraction
- **Typical dependencies:** water supply, flood protection, climate regulation
- **Typical impacts:** land-use change, water pollution (acid drainage, tailings), habitat destruction, air pollution
- **LEAP focus:** mine-site overlap with KBAs / protected areas / indigenous lands, tailings risk, water stress

### 4. Oil & gas
- Upstream extraction, midstream, downstream refining
- **Typical dependencies:** water supply, climate regulation, flood/storm protection (for coastal infrastructure)
- **Typical impacts:** spills, habitat fragmentation, GHG emissions, water pollution, methane leakage
- **LEAP focus:** offshore/onshore sensitive-area overlap, spill history, decommissioning liabilities

### 5. Chemicals
- Basic, specialty, agricultural chemicals
- **Typical dependencies:** water supply, climate regulation, raw materials (feedstocks)
- **Typical impacts:** water pollution, air pollution, persistent chemicals (PFAS), GHG emissions
- **LEAP focus:** discharge permits, downstream agrochemical use, persistent/bioaccumulative substance exposure

### 6. Construction materials
- Cement, aggregates, gypsum, glass
- **Typical dependencies:** raw materials (limestone, sand, aggregates), water supply, climate regulation
- **Typical impacts:** land-use change, water abstraction, air pollution (PM, CO2), habitat loss
- **LEAP focus:** quarry / sand-mining locations, embodied biodiversity loss, decarbonisation roadmap

### 7. Utilities — water & electricity
- Water utilities, electric power generation, transmission, distribution
- **Typical dependencies:** water supply (hydro, thermal cooling), flood protection, climate regulation
- **Typical impacts:** water abstraction and modification, habitat fragmentation (transmission), GHG (fossil generation), thermal pollution
- **LEAP focus:** generation mix, water-stressed basin exposure, fish passage, grid in sensitive corridors

### 8. Real estate
- Commercial, residential, REITs, developers
- **Typical dependencies:** flood/storm protection, climate regulation, water supply
- **Typical impacts:** land-use change, sealing/impervious surfaces, urban biodiversity loss, embodied biodiversity in materials
- **LEAP focus:** flood and water-stress exposure, biodiversity-net-gain regimes (e.g., UK BNG), green infrastructure

### 9. Infrastructure
- Roads, rail, ports, airports, telecoms, dams, pipelines
- **Typical dependencies:** flood/storm protection, water supply, climate regulation
- **Typical impacts:** habitat fragmentation, land-use change, hydrological modification, noise & light pollution
- **LEAP focus:** linear-infrastructure crossings of KBAs / protected areas / migration corridors

### 10. Transportation
- Shipping, aviation, road freight, rail, automakers
- **Typical dependencies:** climate regulation (storm windows, ice-free routes), water supply (manufacturing)
- **Typical impacts:** GHG emissions, marine pollution (ballast, plastics, noise, oil), invasive species transport, air pollution
- **LEAP focus:** shipping route biodiversity exposure, invasive-species pathway risk, EV battery supply-chain nature footprint

### 11. Biotech & pharmaceuticals
- Drug discovery, manufacturing, medical devices
- **Typical dependencies:** genetic resources (bioprospecting), water supply, climate regulation
- **Typical impacts:** water pollution (pharmaceutical residues, APIs), waste, packaging
- **LEAP focus:** Nagoya Protocol / access-and-benefit-sharing compliance, effluent management, supply-chain nature footprint

### 12. Apparel & textiles
- Cotton and synthetic fibre production, dyeing, finishing, apparel manufacturing, footwear
- **Typical dependencies:** water supply, soil quality, raw materials (natural fibres, leather, dyes)
- **Typical impacts:** water abstraction, water pollution (dyes, microfibres), land-use change (cotton, leather), GHG
- **LEAP focus:** tier-N supply-chain water and chemical use, deforestation-linked leather, microfibre release

### 13. Consumer goods
- Household & personal products, packaged foods (overlaps with #1), tobacco, cosmetics
- **Typical dependencies:** raw materials (palm oil, soy, paper, water), pollination, soil quality
- **Typical impacts:** deforestation-linked sourcing, plastic and packaging pollution, water and chemical use
- **LEAP focus:** commodity-driven deforestation (palm, soy, cocoa, coffee, paper), plastic packaging exposure, EUDR

### 14. Financials — banks, insurers, asset managers, asset owners (transmission belt)
- Commercial banks, investment banks, P&C insurance, life insurance, asset managers, asset owners (including fund-of-funds LPs)
- **Typical dependencies:** indirect — exposure runs through financed/insured/owned activities
- **Typical impacts:** indirect — capital allocation enables real-economy impacts; underwriting enables operations
- **LEAP focus:** financed-emissions-style nature exposure aggregation, sectoral concentration, sensitive-location concentration, engagement and stewardship effectiveness

## How to use this list

1. At LEAP-Locate, screen the deal's sub-industry against this list. If it's in one, default to deeper LEAP.
2. Even if not on this list, use the "typical dependencies/impacts" rows as a checklist — many "non-priority" sectors have hidden upstream exposure (e.g., tech hardware = mining + chemicals + water).
3. For financials, run LEAP through to the underlying real-economy exposures — that's where the actual nature interface sits.
