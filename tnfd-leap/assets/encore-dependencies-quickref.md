# ENCORE — sector dependency & impact quick reference

ENCORE (Exploring Natural Capital Opportunities, Risks & Exposure) is the joint UNEP-FI / UNEP-WCMC / Global Canopy tool that rates the materiality of each economic sub-industry's dependence on, and impact via, specific ecosystem services and pressures. It is the most-cited starting point for TNFD LEAP-Evaluate.

This reference gives the high-level matrix and how to use the live tool. Always cross-check against the live ENCORE database — ratings are periodically updated.

## How ENCORE structures the world

- **Sub-industries** based on the Global Industry Classification Standard (GICS) / SASB sub-industries.
- **Ecosystem services** — the things businesses depend on (e.g., surface water, ground water, soil quality, pollination, climate regulation, flood/storm protection, fibres & other materials, water flow maintenance, bio-remediation, dilution by atmosphere and ecosystems, mass stabilisation and erosion control).
- **Pressures on nature** — the things businesses do that drive nature change (e.g., terrestrial ecosystem use, freshwater ecosystem use, marine ecosystem use, water use, soil pollutants, water pollutants, solid waste, GHG emissions, non-GHG air pollutants, disturbances).
- **Materiality ratings** per sub-industry per service/pressure: Very Low / Low / Medium / High / Very High.

- This is a coarse summary — when the deal warrants action, query ENCORE directly for the precise sub-industry and pressure.

## How to use ENCORE in a LEAP-Evaluate workflow

1. **Pick the GICS sub-industry** (or sub-industries — many portcos straddle two).
2. **Pull ENCORE's dependency ratings** for that sub-industry. Note any "High" or "Very High" services.
3. **Pull ENCORE's pressure (impact) ratings** for the same sub-industry. Note any "High" or "Very High" pressures.
4. **Cross-walk to TNFD's five impact drivers**:
   - Terrestrial / freshwater / marine ecosystem use → driver 1 (land/sea use change)
   - Water use → driver 2 (direct exploitation)
   - GHG emissions → driver 3 (climate change)
   - Soil/water/solid waste/air pollutants → driver 4 (pollution)
   - (ENCORE does not separately rate invasive species — supplement from sectoral literature for driver 5)
5. **Localise**: ENCORE ratings are global averages. Adjust upward if the deal's geography intensifies the pressure (e.g., water dependence in a high-stress basin per WRI Aqueduct) or sensitivity (e.g., operations in a KBA per IBAT).
6. **Document the screen**: list dependencies and impacts rated Medium or higher, with ENCORE as the source, and what supplementary data (location, supply chain) was used to refine.

## Caveats

- ENCORE is a screening tool, not a measurement tool. It tells you *where to look*, not *how much*. Don't quote ENCORE materiality ratings as evidence of actual impact magnitude.
- Ratings are sub-industry-level averages. A best-in-class operator in a "Very High" sector may have materially lower exposure; a laggard in a "Medium" sector may have higher.
- ENCORE's coverage is strongest for terrestrial and freshwater; marine and atmospheric coverage is improving but thinner.
- Always pair with site-level data from IBAT, Aqueduct, Global Forest Watch, or commissioned baselines before making material claims.
