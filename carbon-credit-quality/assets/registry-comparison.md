# Registry comparison — Verra / Gold Standard / ART / ACR / CAR / Plan Vivo

Use this to calibrate expectations by registry. Registry choice is necessary-but-not-sufficient: a good registry can still issue weak credits if the underlying methodology and project are weak. A weaker registry can still host strong individual projects. Always diligence at the project level too.

## Verra (VCS — Verified Carbon Standard)

- **Volume:** Largest VCM registry by issuance volume.
- **Coverage:** Broadest — AFOLU (ARR, IFM, REDD+, ALM, blue carbon), engineered removals, industrial, energy, transport, waste.
- **Strengths:** Methodology breadth; mature infrastructure; large secondary market liquidity; co-benefit add-on standards (CCB for biodiversity & community, SD VISta for SDG impact).
- **Weaknesses:** Legacy REDD+ stockpile (VM0007 family) has been widely criticised for systematic over-crediting; reputational risk from media investigations (2023 onwards).
- **Reform trajectory:** VM0048 consolidated REDD+ methodology; new ARR methodology VM0047; methodology consolidation across AFOLU; ICVCM engagement.
- **Use when:** Project type fits available methodologies; investor accepts the legacy-stockpile reputational dimension; counterparty due-diligence verifies post-reform methodology version.

## Gold Standard

- **Volume:** Smaller than Verra; concentrated in cookstoves, water, community renewables, and SDG-impact-themed projects.
- **Strengths:** Tighter project-level review; strong SDG-impact framing (every project must demonstrate ≥3 SDG contributions with indicators); reputation premium reflected in pricing.
- **Weaknesses:** Cookstove portfolio carries the same usage-rate-assumption concerns affecting that project type sector-wide; volumes are smaller, so liquidity for any one vintage / methodology can be thin.
- **Use when:** Project explicitly aims to demonstrate SDG co-benefits; buyer is willing to pay premium for governance reputation.

## ART (Architecture for REDD+ Transactions) / TREES

- **Volume:** Newer; ramping with LEAF Coalition demand.
- **Coverage:** Jurisdictional REDD+ only (national or large sub-national programs).
- **Strengths:** Jurisdictional anchoring structurally addresses the baseline-inflation criticisms levelled at project-scale REDD+; aligned with major sovereign-buyer demand (LEAF Coalition); strong governance design.
- **Weaknesses:** Issuance pace constrained by jurisdictional readiness (national MRV, safeguards, registries); not applicable to project-scale work.
- **Use when:** Underwriting jurisdictional REDD+ transactions; building exposure to sovereign-aligned VCM segments.

## ACR (American Carbon Registry)

- **Volume:** Mid-size; US-anchored.
- **Coverage:** Strong on methane (livestock, landfill, coal mine), IFM in US contexts, ODS destruction.
- **Strengths:** Long operating history (founded 1996); accepted under California compliance program for certain protocols (creating compliance-VCM linkages); rigorous protocol development.
- **Weaknesses:** Geographic concentration; less methodology breadth outside US-relevant project types.
- **Use when:** US methane, US forestry, ODS destruction projects; investor wants compliance-market crossover potential.

## CAR (Climate Action Reserve)

- **Volume:** Mid-size; US and Mexico focus.
- **Coverage:** US forestry, US/Mexico methane, ODS, soil enrichment, organic waste.
- **Strengths:** Respected protocol development process; accepted under California compliance program; strong on US forestry MRV.
- **Weaknesses:** Like ACR, geographically concentrated; some legacy California IFM projects flagged by independent researchers (notably CarbonPlan) on common-practice baselines.
- **Use when:** US-Mexico project types; compliance-market crossover relevance.

## Plan Vivo

- **Volume:** Small.
- **Coverage:** Smallholder and community-led projects, frequently in low-income and forest-frontier geographies. ARR, agroforestry, blue carbon, community forestry.
- **Strengths:** Strongest registry on community governance and benefit-sharing requirements; outcomes credible; high co-benefit density.
- **Weaknesses:** Ex-ante issuance model (Plan Vivo Certificates issued on projected sequestration, with verification cycles) carries different risk profile than ex-post issuance; small volumes mean liquidity is thin; lower brand recognition with corporate buyers.
- **Use when:** Impact-thesis-driven buyers prioritising community outcomes; small-scale community forestry / agroforestry deals; willing to accept thinner liquidity for higher integrity profile.

## Cross-registry comparison — quick reference

| Dimension | Verra | Gold Standard | ART/TREES | ACR | CAR | Plan Vivo |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Volume | Largest | Mid | Small-growing | Mid | Mid | Small |
| Geography | Global | Global | National-scale | US-focused | US/Mexico | Smallholder global |
| Methodology breadth | Broadest | Medium | REDD+ only | Medium | Medium | Narrow |
| Co-benefit framing | Add-on (CCB, SD VISta) | Built-in (SDGs) | Built-in (safeguards) | Limited | Limited | Built-in (community) |
| Issuance model | Ex-post | Ex-post | Ex-post | Ex-post | Ex-post | Ex-ante PVCs |
| Compliance crossover | Some | Limited | LEAF / sovereign | California CCO | California CCO | None |
| Legacy issuance concerns | REDD+ VM0007 | Cookstoves | Limited (new) | Limited | Some IFM | Limited |

## How to use this file

1. Identify the issuing registry from the project documentation.
2. Note the registry-level strengths and weaknesses.
3. Combine with the methodology-flags assessment and the project-type-specific concerns to score CCPs.
4. Where a project is dual-listed or has a co-benefit add-on certification (e.g., VCS + CCB), score the co-benefit standard separately.
