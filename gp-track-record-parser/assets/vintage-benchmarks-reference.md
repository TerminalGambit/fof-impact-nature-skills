# Vintage benchmarks reference — top-quartile and median thresholds

**Refresh annually.** Numbers below are approximate institutional consensus bands as of the most recent full-year cycle. Sources: Cambridge Associates Benchmarks, Preqin Performance Analyst, MSCI Burgiss Private iQ. Use as orientation, not as a quotable benchmark — for an IC memo, pull the live percentile from the named source.

## How to read this table

- Numbers are net-to-LP unless stated.
- Vintage = year of first capital call (not fund closing).
- Quartile thresholds are the *boundary* — top-quartile threshold means the 75th percentile fund returned at or above that figure.
- "Mature" = fund age ≥ 8 years. Younger vintages are still moving and should be benchmarked on DPI separately from TVPI.
- Provider disagreement of 200–400 bps on IRR thresholds is normal. If three sources agree within a tight band, treat the placement as high-confidence.

## Natural Capital / Timberland / Farmland

Benchmark against NCREIF Timberland Index and NCREIF Farmland Index, not against PE or equities. Closed-end natural capital funds have a short institutional history and dispersion data is thin.

Approximate ranges for mature vintages (2010–2015), closed-end natural capital strategies:
- Top-quartile Net IRR: 9–12%
- Median Net IRR: 5–7%
- DPI: rarely above 1.0x within first 8 years — these are duration assets

Caution: many natural capital funds have not yet returned a full cycle of capital. Vintage benchmarking is provisional. Lean on PME vs NCREIF Timberland/Farmland and direct alpha rather than peer quartiles for this asset class.

## Impact / ESG-labeled (cross-strategy)

There is no clean vintage benchmark for "impact" — it is a label across strategies. Benchmark each impact fund against the underlying strategy's vintage peer set (a regenerative ag PE fund against PE vintages, not against "impact funds" as a category). The "impact penalty / premium" question is empirical and contested — do not assume either direction without the data.

## Provider quirks to remember

- **Cambridge Associates** publishes pooled returns (capital-weighted) and median; calculations include both realized and unrealized. Strong on US, weaker on Asia.
- **Preqin** publishes manager-weighted and capital-weighted; very broad universe but cash flow detail is patchier than Burgiss.
- **Burgiss / MSCI Private Capital** has the cleanest cash-flow-level dataset (LP-sourced); used by many consultants and DFIs. Slightly smaller universe than Preqin.
- **Pitchbook** strong on venture deal-level data; performance benchmarks are useful but less institutional than Cambridge / Burgiss.

When sources disagree on quartile placement, the disagreement itself is a finding — report it. Do not silently pick the most favorable.
